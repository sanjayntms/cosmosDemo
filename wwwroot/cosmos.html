📖 Core Concepts & Visual Flow

This tool simulates how data propagates across a globally distributed database (like Azure Cosmos DB) under different architectural rules known as Consistency Models.

The Consistency Models

How does the database balance speed vs. accuracy?

  • Strong: Highest accuracy, slowest speed. The system refuses to confirm the write to the user until the data is successfully copied to every single region around the world.
  • Session (Default): The perfect middle ground. It guarantees "Read Your Own Writes." The local write is instant, and background replication to other regions happens extremely fast, making it ideal for most modern applications.
  • Eventual: Highest speed, lowest accuracy. The local write is instant, but the system makes no guarantees about when the remote regions will be updated. They will simply catch up "eventually."
How to read the Visual Flow

When you click "Write Data", observe the sequence of events:

1. The Trigger: You act as an application located in Sydney, sending a piece of data to the local Australian database.
2. The Local Write: Watch how quickly the Australia node updates. Notice that under Strong consistency, even the local node freezes until global sync finishes!
3. Background Replication: Watch the numbers flash green as the data travels over the fiber-optic network to India and the US. Notice the drastic delay differences between Session and Eventual rules.
